What Do I Need to Consider When Choosing a Dog Training Collar?

There are some key features to keep in mind...

Training Collar Correction Type

By far, most remote dog training collars come with a static/electric correction component. This is certainly essential for working and hunting dogs. Most will also have a tone/vibration option available.

For more timid pet dogs, a citronella remote dog training collar is also available and we have a vibration only training collar that is an excellent option for deaf dogs.

Range

This is a vital consideration before purchasing a dog training collar. How far could you be away from your dog when you press the button? Always try and work out the worst-case scenario as this determines the minimum range capability you may need.

Always select a remote training collar that has more range than the minimum range capability you need.

Durability

What you are using your remote training collar for will determine how robust it needs to be. Around the home obedience will not require the same rugged standards as a farmer with working dogs or a pig hunter.

Waterproof collars and handpieces may also be important if your dog can access water or when using a collar system in wet conditions.
